Events Module Tabs

There are three new tabs in the Events module that allow you to keep track of breakdowns, service issues, and emergencies that occur during your agency’s operations. The process for creating these records is fully explained below.

How to Create a Breakdown, Service Issue, or Emergency Event

The Breakdowns tab allows you to keep records of any time one of your vehicles break down that occur. Complete the following steps to create a breakdown incident record:

  1. Open the Events module and click the New button on the toolbar.
  2. Select New Breakdown Incident from the drop-down menu. This loads a new incident record on the screen.

  1. Enter any necessary information into the fields in accordance with your agency’s policies. Note that any field with a red arrow next to them are required. Each of these fields are fully explained below:
  • Date – Enter or select the date of the accident from the drop-down calendar.
  • Vehicle – The vehicle the operator was driving when the breakdown took place.
  • Type – Your agency may use this field to organize their breakdown incident records. The available options are created in the Settings module under Incident Options.
  • Time – The time during the selected date when this incident occurred.
  • Needs Attention – Check this box if it requires attention from your agency’s staff. Note that it is possible to filter the Event module’s records for those that need attention, so you can find them easily.
  • Contacted – Indicate whether this incident required medical or police involvement by clicking the corresponding checkboxes.
  • Police Report ID – If this incident resulted in a police report, you can record its number here.
  • Vehicle Pool – The pool that includes the driver’s vehicle.
  • Driver – Enter or select the name of the driver who was carrying out the route when the accident took place.
  • Service Delay – The amount of delay caused by this incident.
  • Run – The run the driver was working during the incident.
  • Details – Any particulars about the incident that your agency wishes to note in the record.
  • Manager Notified – The manager at your agency who was informed of this incident.
  • Person Assigned – The agency staff member who is currently assigned to resolve this incident.

 

The Attachments button is disabled when creating a new entry. An accident must be saved before attachments can be added to it. For more information, refer to the Attachments section of this chapter.

  1. You can then note any customers who were involved in this incident by clicking the New button in the “Additional Customers Involved” section of the screen. This opens the Select Customer window, which you can use to find the customer, indicate if they were injured, and enter any important comments.
  2. Add any necessary notes to the record by clicking the New button in the Notes section of the screen. This opens the Create Note window which allows you to enter the note’s text (click OK when finished). The Edited By field automatically populates with your RouteMatch user name for future reference.
  3. Once you have entered all necessary information into the incident’s record, click Save on the toolbar.
